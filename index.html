About SecureID™

Established 2007 · Definitely a real company

Our Mission

SecureID™ exists to bring frictionless identity verification to the masses — or more accurately, to bring the masses' identities to us. We believe every person deserves to hand their government-issued documents to a stranger on the internet, confident that something will happen to them afterward.

Our Story

2007 Founded in a garage. ISO 27001 certification application submitted. Never heard back.
2011 Upgraded from SHA-1 to SHA-1 with a comment that says "TODO: upgrade this".
2016 First data breach. We sent a strongly-worded internal Slack message about it.
2018 GDPR became enforceable. We added a cookie banner. Problem solved.
2021 Migrated user data to a public GitHub repository for "scalability reasons".
2024 Integrated AI verification. The AI also doesn't know what it's doing, but it's confident about it.
2025 Received our 4th "Most Innovative Startup" award from an organisation we've never heard of.
2026ff We are optimistic something will happen soon.

Last updated: Now — if not, read again · Effective: Immediately · Applies to: You, specifically

The Short Version

We collect everything. We store everything. We share… some things.

By visiting this page you have agreed to our full terms. Scrolling down constitutes a legally binding signature in 34 jurisdictions we just made up.

1. What We Collect

We collect any information you provide, any information you don't provide but we infer, and any information decides to hallucinate. This includes but is not limited to:

  • Your name, date of birth, address, and document number (from your ID)
  • Your password (hashed, unless the dev box is running, in which case plaintext)
  • The face on your ID photo, processed by an AI that will remember it forever
  • Ambient audio (currently disabled — but the checkbox is in the code, commented out)
  • Your hopes, dreams, and reasonable expectations of privacy

2. How We Store It

Your data is stored in a highly secure infrastructure — specifically a public GitHub repository with a descriptive folder name. We chose GitHub because it has a green "Verified" badge next to commits, which we felt communicated trust.

Backups are maintained in three locations: the main repo, the refs/heads/main branch, and Nyxie's USB-stick.

4. Your Rights (EU/UK)

Under GDPR you have the right to access, correct, and delete your data. To exercise these rights, please submit a written request by post to our registered address. We do not have a registered address. Requests sent digitally will be filtered by a rule titled "ignore".

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority. We encourage you to do this, as we find the letters entertaining.

5. Cookies

We use all cookies. First-party, third-party, session, persistent, analytical, marketing, and some we invented ourselves called "investigative cookies" which are not a recognised category but felt accurate. By existing on the internet you have consented to all of them.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy at any time, retroactively, including for data we collected before the policy existed. We will notify you of changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page (we won't).
